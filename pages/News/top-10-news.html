Whether it's the lure of achieving the top-notch education or the dreams of settling down in a progressive nation, the path to fulfill these abound with paperwork, visas, work concessions, and permanent residency processes. Recognizing such needs, several countries around the globe have tailored their PR rules and regulations to cater to international students, particularly from India.

Canada's Points-Based System

The north American nation has been consistently ranking high in the list of coveted destinations for higher education and is equally receptive to international students when it comes to PR. Canada operates a points-based system, known as Express Entry, where factors like age, education, relevant work experience, and language abilities are evaluated. Higher scores lead to a better chance of receiving an invitation to apply for PR.

Germany's Job Seeker Visas

Germany offers the job seeker visa, allowing international students to remain in the country for up to 18 months post graduation while they look for a job. This helps them transition to a work permit or EU Blue Card later, which can eventually open the path to permanent residency.
