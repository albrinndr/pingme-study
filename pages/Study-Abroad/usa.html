  • Electric: 50 – 100 USD/month, depending on the size of your apartment, and if your heating is electric, the overall price may go up to 150 USD/month.

  • Heating: 50 – 100 USD/month.

  • Internet averages 45-50 USD/month.

  • Most phone services average 50 USD/month.

Admission requirements

Each university in the U.S. has its own system on their website that allows future students to apply for and enroll in their degree programs. So, once you select the University of your choice, you can go through their application and submit your materials there.

Documents required for admission

Here are some of the basic requirements needed for the application procedure to study in USA for Indian students:

  • A scanned copy of your diploma certificate (high school or Bachelor’s degree)

  • A transcript/record of your previous courses

  • A scan of your passport

  • Your CV

  • Testing scores

  • Evidence of scholarship or funding

  • Letters of recommendation (between 2-3)

  • Portfolio and/or writing samples

To apply to study in the U.S., you’ll need to provide language certificate as a proof of your English proficiency.

The certificates generally accepted by the universities in U.S. are:

  • TOEFL

  • IELTS

  • CAE or CPE

Still, you should always check on your university’s website, just to be sure what the exact requirements are.

Intakes & Deadlines

U.S Universities have two intakes for admission purposes, namely Fall and Spring semesters. Fall semester starts in late August and ends in late December or early January whereas the Spring semester begins in January and ends in early May.

Obtaining a student visa in the United States of America is not an easy task. You will have to undergo a lot of documentation work and applications for visa services for the USA. Unlike other countries, your visa is not guaranteed even if you get an admission confirmation from a USA university. You will have to pass the visa guidelines and requirements. The following are the procedures to initialize visa services to the USA.

  1. Obtain I-20 from University / College

  2. Obtain SEVIS FEE Receipt

  3. Complete DS-160 Form

  4. Pay Visa Application Fees

  5. Schedule Appointment

  6. Attend Appointment Scheduled

Documents for visa

  1. A valid passport

  2. The Non-immigrant Visa Application, Form DS-160

  3. The application fee payment receipt

  4. A passport photo

  5. A Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant (F-1) Student Status (Form 1-20)
