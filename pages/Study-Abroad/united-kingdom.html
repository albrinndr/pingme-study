Tuition fees & Costs

Average Master’s degree tuition fees in the United Kingdom range from 6,000 – 10,000 GBP per year. However, for some specialized degrees, such as Medicine, Law or Business, you should expect higher tuition fees.

If you are an EU/EEA student, you will be charged the same tuition as UK students, while the average tuition fees charged to non-EU students range at 17,500 GBP/year

Admission requirements

You can either apply for 5 courses in the same university or the same course in 5 universities. Each university has its way of handling the applications. Therefore, the requirements depend on the universities. The following are the steps.

  1. Registering with UCAS. You have to create an account with your details

  2. Choose desirable courses. You can use the search portal to find suitable courses and also note the UCAS code to fill out the application.

  3. Find your university. Once you decided on the courses, you have to list down the universities in which you want to apply.

  4. Fill out the application form. You can apply for only 5, and you have to give 5 UCAS code for the courses and universities and fill in the application form.

  5. Send and wait for the replies. When you send the application form, you are done. You have to wait for replies now.

Generally, international students have to apply between June 30 and September 1 of the preceding year of commencing classes.

Documents required for admission

Some of the documents for applying are:

  • Copy of passport

  • Degree certificates

  • Higher education certificates

  • Personal Statement/ Motivation Letter

  • CV

  • Recommendation letters

  • Certificates of any other courses you have attended

  • English proficiency certificates – IELTS/TOEFL/CAE. Scores depend on the university

Intakes & Deadlines

Unlike a single intake in Indian universities, colleges and universities in the UK offer two intakes.

Term 1: September to December

Term 2: January to April

Summer: April to June

Financial requirements

A bank-slip, bank statement or certificate of deposit that shows you have held enough money to cover your course fees and your monthly living costs for up to 9 months is required for study in UK for Indian students.
