Study In Czech Republic

The Czech Republic, with its distinct range of study programmes, has a long tradition in providing quality education. It is one among the safest countries in the world with rich culture and fantastic travel hubs and you can presumably obtain an education loan for abroad studies to the Czech Republic.

Questions

An opportunity to experience their amazing culture

Rather than just formal education, the country is offering a lot more to international students. With the great history of amazing cultures and social conditions, the country is a great home for international students to pursue their higher education. The oldest university in central Europe, named Charles University, is in this country.

World-famous universities with excellent academic track records.

There are several other famous universities, including Masaryk University and the Czech Technical University. All of them are world-famous and have been attracting thousands of students each year from different countries. Academical excellence of the country is much better than some of the leading European countries.

Higher education is completely free

The higher education in the country is completely free by law, and you won’t be charged any tuition fees if you study in any of the public universities. This offer is available for both domestic and international students without any limitations.

Affordable living costs

The living cost of the country is very affordable for everyone, and you can also apply for an International Student Identity Card (ISIC). It will give you great discounts at restaurants, book stores, and other places.

Plenty of scholarships

International students are eligible for several scholarships, including those offered by specific institutions and the European Union. The Czech government offers financial aid scholarships to study in Czech Republic for Indian students and other international students through the Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport, and within the framework of the Foreign Development Aid.

The Czech degree structure is similar to that of the United States. Degree programs are as follows:

  • Bachelor Degree ( 3 to 4 years)

  • Master Degree ( 4 to 6 years)

  • Doctoral ( usually 3 years)

This country has over 70 public and private academic institutions for higher education with programs in the Czech language designed specifically for international students. There are also plenty of English-taught programs. These days, over 37,000 international students are studying in the Czech Republic as the university is continuing to provide English-taught programs.

Tuition fees & costs

The average tuition fee for one academic year is about 3 000 EUR. Studying medicine in the Czech Republic will cost you about 10 000 EUR per academic year. The tuition fee for the MBA degree program would vary from 5 000 EUR to 35 000 EUR depending on the program.

Admission requirements

Applications to universities in the Czech Republic is an independent process. Each university has its procedure, and you have to visit the respective websites to know more. You have to shortlist universities first and then check for the admission requirements.

Documents required for admission

Some of the documents required for the application are:

  • A scan of your diploma (high school or Bachelor’s degree)

  • A transcript/record of your previous courses

  • A scan of your passport and birth certificate

  • Your CV

  • Testing scores

  • Evidence of scholarship or funding

  • Letters of recommendation (between 2-3)

  • Portfolio and writing samples

You have to provide proof of your English Proficiency. Below given are the accepted tests:

  • TOEFL

  • IELTS

  • CAE or CPE

Intakes & Deadlines

The application deadline is mostly between February and April. Students may apply for several study programs at various universities and faculties.
