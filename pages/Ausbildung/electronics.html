Electronics Ausbildung

This 3-year course in electronics covers a wide array of essential skills and information in the field. Through this course, you will delve into various aspects of electronics, including circuit design, electronic components, troubleshooting, and system integration.

Gain expertise in areas such as digital and analog electronics, microcontroller programming, and electronic systems maintenance. This program equips you with the practical skills and theoretical understanding necessary for a successful career in electronics.

Minimum Academic Qualification : Plus-two

German Language Proficiency : B1/B2 Complete Modules

Tuition Fees : None

Duration : 3 Years

Stipend : Available, around €800/month in first year

  • Understand circuit diagrams and install electrical components correctly

  • Know safety precautions and the importance of being cautious with electricity at the workplace

  • Installing tested security technology to protect users unfamiliar with electronics

  • Programming and setting up computers to control various industrial processes

  • Handling state-of-the-art technology used in manufacturing facilities

  • Repairing, checking, and adjusting machines, all of which operate on electricity

Once you complete your ausbildung in electronics, you can find job opportunities as an electronics technician in Germany and across Europe.

You can also choose from a wide variety of topics ranging from electronics, mechatronics and electrical energy technology to electrical measurement, control and regulation technology, for further professional training. After this, you can qualify to be an industrial-technical manager and can lead employees.

Proficiency in the German language is a crucial requirement for joining an IT ausbildung program. Applicants must have a B2 level of German language skills, as the communication at work and the training place will be mostly in German.

Some employers might even accept applicants with a B1 level of proficiency after a language assessment interview.

The most attractive aspect of the ausbildung programs in Germany is that there is no tuition fee. Instead, they provide monthly stipends to the participants.

During the first year of ausbildung, students can expect a gross salary ranging from 700 to 930 euros per month. You will get this stipend from the first month itself. Typically, there’s an increase of approximately 50 euros in the second year. In the third year, the raise is usually around 100 euros, and during the last six months, you can expect to earn between 850 and 1,120 euros per month.
